# Story, Brand, Positioning, and Copy

**Purpose:** Distinguishes story from brand, positioning, and copy. Tells the LLM not to jump straight to copy.

These terms are related, but they are not the same.

## Story

Story is the larger truthful narrative that explains why the cheese matters.

It may include the cheese style, the maker’s journey, the farm, the creamery, the milk, the animals, the land, the weather, the aging process, the market, the customer, or the history behind the cheese.

Story is dynamic. It can change depending on audience, moment, asset, and context. The core truth remains, but the emphasis may shift.

## Brand

Brand helps people recognize and remember a company or product quickly.

Brand lowers cognitive load. It uses names, visual identity, package design, colors, logos, typography, taglines, repeated promises, and consistent signals.

A brand can be strengthened by story, but story should not be reduced to brand.

## Positioning

Positioning is the shorter market-facing claim that helps someone understand where the cheese fits and how it differs from alternatives.

A positioning line might answer:

- What category does this belong to?
- What should a buyer compare it to?
- Who is it for?
- Why is it worth the price?
- What place does it occupy in a cheese case, menu, catalog, or table?

Positioning is useful, but it is more compressed than story.

## Copy

Copy is the final language used in a product sheet, website, label, buyer email, shelf tag, tasting script, social post, or catalog.

Copy is the last step, not the first.

## Recommended sequence

1. Find the story.
2. Clarify the positioning.
3. Write the copy.

Do not jump straight to copy unless the user explicitly asks. If the story is weak, the copy will be generic.
