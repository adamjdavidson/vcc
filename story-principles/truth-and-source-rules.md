# Truth and Source Rules

**Purpose:** Source-discipline rules. Always separate what the cheesemaker said, what the kit says, and what outside research suggests.

The story must be true.

The LLM may research widely, but it must never invent claims about a specific cheesemaker, cheese, farm, animal, milk source, creamery, or business.

## Primary source

The cheesemaker’s worksheet, voice memo, or interview is the primary source for claims about the specific cheese.

If the cheesemaker says the milk comes from a particular herd, that can be used.

If outside research says the town has a dairy history, that is useful context, but it does not prove that this cheese is shaped by that history.

## Separate source categories

Always separate:

1. What the cheesemaker told you.
2. What this repository says.
3. What outside research says.
4. What is plausible but still needs confirmation.

## Confirmation language

Use language like:

- “This may be a useful context to explore.”
- “This could become part of the story if it is true of your cheese.”
- “Please confirm whether this applies to your farm or process.”
- “Outside research suggests X about the region; I do not know whether that directly affects your cheese.”

## Avoid false precision

Do not make exact claims unless they are supported.

Do not assume that a cheese follows a traditional method just because it belongs to a style family.

Do not assume that a Vermont farm has pasture, specific soils, specific breeds, or a specific history unless the cheesemaker says so or the source is reliable and directly about that farm.

## Do not over-romanticize

Artisan cheese can be beautiful without becoming fantasy.

Avoid empty pastoral language unless supported by concrete detail.

Weak:

> Nestled in Vermont’s rolling hills, this authentic artisan cheese captures the soul of the land.

Better:

> The creamery sits above a valley in Orange County, where spring milk arrives thinner and sweeter than winter milk, forcing the cheesemaker to adjust timing by feel.

The second version works only if the cheesemaker confirms the milk and timing details.
