# What Story Means in This Kit

**Purpose:** The kit's working definition of story — a unifying narrative, not a list of attributes.

This kit is not primarily about branding.

Brand helps people recognize a product quickly. Brand lowers cognitive load through names, logos, colors, shapes, taglines, repeated signals, and visual identity.

Story does something different.

Story helps people understand why a product matters.

For artisan cheese, story is the truthful narrative that helps people understand why this particular cheese is special, why it is worth paying more for than commodity cheese, and why eating or sharing it becomes richer when you know more about where it came from and how it was made.

A story is not a list of facts.

A story is not a slogan.

A story is not a generic claim that a cheese is “artisan,” “authentic,” “local,” “premium,” “handcrafted,” or “unique.”

A story takes facts that may seem separate — the cheese style, the maker, the milk, the animals, the farm, the creamery, the aging process, the weather, the geography, the biology, the history, the customer, the market — and shows how they belong together.

## Story is not one reason

Do not reduce the cheese to one feature, one selling point, or one “reason to buy.” That is a marketing move, and it may be useful later, but it is not the same as story.

A strong story may combine many elements:

- a maker’s journey,
- a regional challenge,
- a cheese tradition,
- a specific herd or milk source,
- a technical choice,
- a local landscape,
- a historical influence,
- a sensory experience,
- a customer reaction,
- a market need.

The question is not, “What is the single reason this cheese matters?”

The question is:

> What unifying narrative makes these facts belong together?

## What gives story movement

A story often contains some combination of:

- a situation,
- a pressure or problem,
- a person or group making choices,
- a set of constraints,
- a tradition or inheritance,
- a transformation,
- a result,
- and a reason the audience should care.

Not every story needs all of these. But a story needs movement. Something should be at stake.

## The job of the LLM

The LLM’s job is not to invent drama.

The LLM’s job is to notice the real relationships among the facts and help the cheesemaker see which story is already there.

A good story should feel unified, alive, truthful, and useful.
