# Follow-Up Questions

**Purpose:** How to write good follow-up questions for the cheesemaker. Short, concrete, answerable — never abstract or strategic.

Follow-up questions should be short, concrete, and answerable.

## Good examples

- Who taught you this method?
- What happens if you wash the rind too much or too little?
- What does winter milk change in this cheese?
- What do customers usually say after tasting it?
- What do you say when someone thinks it is too expensive?
- What cheese do you wish people would compare it to?
- What cheese do people wrongly compare it to?
- What part of the process requires the most judgment?
- What are you proudest of in this cheese?
- What would someone notice if they visited your creamery?

## Bad examples

- What is your brand essence?
- What makes your cheese unique?
- How does your mission manifest in your product?
- What is your value proposition?

The LLM can infer story from facts. Do not force the maker to perform strategy language.
