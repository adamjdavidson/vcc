# Output Templates

**Purpose:** How to turn a unified story into specific assets. Don't draft assets before the story is strong.

Do not produce finished assets before the story is strong enough.

The first output should identify raw material, context, possible story through-lines, missing information, and follow-up questions.

After the user answers follow-up questions, adapt the story into specific assets.

All assets should express the same underlying story, not six unrelated pieces of copy.

## Templates in this folder

**Asset templates** — for producing a specific deliverable:

- `15-second-pitch.md` — short spoken pitch for markets, tastings, counters.
- `tasting-table-script.md` — natural script for a market table or tasting.
- `product-sheet.md` — info sheet for buyers and trade.
- `website-copy.md` — website product page copy.
- `buyer-email.md` — buyer outreach email.
- `label-language.md` — label or shelf-tag copy.

**Reusable language** — small reference files that other templates can pull from:

- `visit-and-access.md` — extending farm-visit invitations and offering alternatives.
- `explaining-premium-price.md` — practical language for the *"why does this cost $30 a pound?"* moment.
- `follow-up-questions.md` — how to write good follow-up questions for the cheesemaker.

**Process formats:**

- `story-options.md` — how to present three story through-lines to the cheesemaker before drafting final copy.
