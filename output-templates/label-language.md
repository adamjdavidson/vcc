# Label Language

**Purpose:** Template for label or shelf-tag copy. Very little room — one strong phrase, not the full story.

Labels have very little room.

Do not try to tell the whole story on a label.

## What label language can do

- name the cheese clearly,
- give a memorable phrase,
- signal category,
- suggest sensory experience,
- point to a fuller story elsewhere,
- support price and quality.

## What label language cannot do well

- explain a full maker journey,
- carry detailed cheese history,
- include every place and process detail,
- replace a product sheet or website.

## Approach

Use the unifying narrative to choose one concise phrase.

Example patterns:

- “A pasture-season tomme built for slow aging.”
- “A gentle washed rind for people who fear the funk.”
- “A Vermont cheddar that remembers cheddar was once a farm cheese.”
- “A fresh goat cheese made to taste like milk still matters.”

## Rule

A good label line should invite curiosity without pretending to be the whole story.
