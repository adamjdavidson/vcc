# Tomme

**Purpose:** Story guide for tomme — a flexible category, often associated with rustic farmstead or mountain cheeses.

## Why this style matters

“Tomme” is a broad family term, often associated with rustic, medium-sized, farmstead or mountain cheeses. The category can be flexible, which gives makers room — but also creates storytelling risk because the term may not mean much to general consumers.

## Common characteristics

Possible traits include:

- cow, goat, sheep, or mixed milk,
- natural rind,
- moderate aging,
- earthy, nutty, grassy, milky, mushroomy, or cellar notes,
- semi-firm texture,
- strong relationship to farm and season.

## Story possibilities

A tomme story may involve:

- a practical farm cheese,
- a cheese shaped by milk availability and place,
- a rustic but intentional style,
- the balance between flexibility and tradition,
- the rind and aging room as active forces.

## Questions to ask the maker

- What does “tomme” mean to you?
- What reference cheeses would help a buyer understand it?
- Is the natural rind part of the story?
- What does aging do to it?
- Is it meant to be everyday, special, or both?

## Pitfalls

Do not assume the audience knows the word “tomme.”

Do not use the word as a substitute for description.

Explain the cheese in plain terms before leaning on the category.
