# Blue Cheese

**Purpose:** Story guide for blue cheese — controlled risk, mold as craft partner, the surprise of intensity becoming pleasure.

## Why this style matters

Blue cheeses are ripened with blue molds that create distinctive color, aroma, texture, and flavor. The category ranges from mild, sweet, and creamy to intense, peppery, and crystalline.

Blue cheese is often polarizing, which makes story especially useful.

## Common characteristics

Possible traits include:

- blue or green veining,
- piercing or other oxygen management,
- creamy, crumbly, dense, or fudgy texture,
- sweet, peppery, earthy, mineral, or spicy flavors,
- long aging or special ripening,
- strong need for careful timing and humidity.

## Story possibilities

A blue story may involve:

- controlled risk,
- inviting mold into the cheese and guiding it,
- a dramatic transformation inside the paste,
- turning intensity into pleasure,
- pairing, cooking, or table use,
- the maker’s judgment in deciding when the cheese is ready.

## Questions to ask the maker

- Is your blue mild, intense, sweet, peppery, creamy, crumbly, or something else?
- What do blue-cheese skeptics say when they taste it?
- How do you control veining or ripening?
- How should someone eat it?
- What does it pair with?

## Pitfalls

Do not assume all blue cheeses are aggressive.

Do not describe blue only by intensity.

Do not make mold sound like a gimmick; it is part of the biological craft.
