# Gouda-Style Cheese

**Purpose:** Story guide for Gouda-style cheese — washed-curd technique, transformation through aging, sweetness as approachability.

## Why this style matters

Gouda-style cheeses are associated with Dutch cheesemaking traditions and often involve washed curd, which can produce a sweeter, smoother, less acidic cheese. The category ranges from young, supple cheeses to long-aged, crystalline, caramel-rich versions.

## Common characteristics

Possible traits include:

- cow’s milk most commonly, though other milks are possible,
- washed curd,
- pressed format,
- smooth texture when young,
- caramel, butterscotch, nutty, brothy, or crystalline qualities when aged,
- strong relationship between age and flavor.

## Story possibilities

A Gouda-style story may involve:

- sweetness and approachability,
- transformation through aging,
- the technique of washing curd to shape acidity,
- a cheese that moves from supple to crystalline,
- an entry point for customers who want artisan cheese without intimidation.

## Questions to ask the maker

- Is the cheese young, aged, or very aged?
- Is washed curd part of the method?
- What texture should customers expect?
- Does sweetness matter?
- What do people compare it to?

## Pitfalls

Do not reduce Gouda-style cheese to “mild.”

Do not assume all Goudas are sweet, orange, or waxed.

Do not ignore age: the story of Gouda-style cheese often changes dramatically with time.
