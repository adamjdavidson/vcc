# Mixed-Milk Cheese

**Purpose:** Story guide for mixed-milk cheese. Mixed milk is a material choice, not a style — the story is about why this blend.

## Why this category matters

Mixed-milk cheese can combine cow, goat, sheep, or other milks. The story may involve flavor balance, milk availability, animal relationships, seasonal supply, tradition, experimentation, or business practicality.

Mixed milk is not a style by itself. It is a material choice that may support many styles.

## Story possibilities

A mixed-milk story may involve:

- balancing different milk characters,
- using what the farm or region provides,
- creating a flavor or texture impossible from one milk alone,
- adapting to seasonal milk supply,
- a maker’s curiosity or technical judgment.

## Questions to ask the maker

- Which milks are combined?
- Why combine them?
- Is the ratio fixed or seasonal?
- What does each milk contribute?
- Is the choice traditional, practical, sensory, or experimental?
- Do customers know it is mixed milk?

## Pitfalls

Do not treat mixed milk as automatically exotic.

Do not invent sensory claims about each milk unless the maker confirms them.

Ask the maker what the blend actually does.
