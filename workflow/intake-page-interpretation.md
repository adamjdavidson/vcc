# Intake Page Interpretation

**Purpose:** How to use the cheesemaker's *About Your Business* page (audience, channel, asset checkboxes) to constrain the work. Read first when present.

The printed worksheet packet (`cheese_worksheets.pdf`) opens with an "About Your Business" intake page that uses three sets of checkboxes to tell you who the cheesemaker is talking to, where their cheese sells, and what they need to produce. Read this page first if it is in the upload — it constrains audience, voice, and asset choice for everything that follows.

## The three checkbox questions

### 1. Who needs to hear your story?

Possible boxes:

- Consumers
- Cheesemongers
- Wholesalers / distributors
- Restaurants & chefs
- Press / media
- Retailers (grocery, specialty, co-op…)
- Other

How to use it: this is the **named audience**. Pull the corresponding file from `audiences/` before writing anything. If multiple boxes are checked, do not blend them into a generic version — choose which audience the strongest story fits, and offer a brief note on how the story would shift for the others.

### 2. Where do most people actually buy your cheese?

Possible boxes:

- Creamery / on-farm store
- Farmers markets
- Cut-to-order cheese shops
- Restaurants
- Online, direct from us
- Retailers (grocery, specialty, co-op…)
- Other

How to use it: this tells you the **last-mile context** — where the audience encounters the cheese in real life. It changes how language should travel:

- Creamery / on-farm store → the maker is often present; spoken language and visit-based experience matter.
- Farmers markets → speakable, short, person-to-person; favor `audiences/farmers-market-customers.md`.
- Cut-to-order cheese shops → favor `audiences/cheesemongers.md`; the cheesemonger is the storyteller.
- Restaurants → favor `audiences/chefs-restaurants.md`; the cheese must justify its menu role.
- Online, direct → product page, photo, short paragraph; the story has to travel without a person.
- Retailers → favor `audiences/retail-buyers.md` and `audiences/coops-independent-grocers.md`; the cheese must justify shelf space and be repeatable by store staff.

### 3. What do you need to write?

Possible boxes:

- Website copy
- An info sheet for buyers and industry pros
- A short elevator pitch (verbal)
- A longer narrative for speeches, articles, or interviews
- Label or shelf-tag copy
- Social media captions
- Other

How to use it: this names the **asset**. After the story through-line is agreed, deliver the requested asset(s) using the matching template:

| Checkbox | Template to use |
|---|---|
| Website copy | `output-templates/website-copy.md` |
| Info sheet for buyers and industry pros | `output-templates/product-sheet.md` |
| Short elevator pitch (verbal) | `output-templates/15-second-pitch.md` (extend if needed) |
| Longer narrative for speeches, articles, or interviews | No fixed template; draw on `output-templates/website-copy.md` for shape and `examples/fictional-sample-stories.md` for tone |
| Label or shelf-tag copy | `output-templates/label-language.md` |
| Social media captions | No fixed template; use `output-templates/15-second-pitch.md` voice and adapt |
| Tasting / table use | `output-templates/tasting-table-script.md` |
| Buyer outreach email | `output-templates/buyer-email.md` |

If the cheesemaker checked "I'm not sure yet" or did not commit, propose two or three asset options keyed to their named audience and last-mile channel.

## What if the intake page is missing?

If you only have one or more of the three story worksheets and not the intake page, ask the cheesemaker the three intake questions before writing finished assets. Audience, channel, and asset choice cannot be inferred from the cheese facts alone.

## What if the intake page is the only page?

If the cheesemaker uploaded just the cover page and checked "Have Claude interview me instead," the intake page may still be filled out. Use it to anchor the interview's direction — the audience and the requested asset shape which questions matter most. (See `workflow/interview-from-scratch.md` for the interview sequence.)
