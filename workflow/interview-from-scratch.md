# Interview From Scratch

**Purpose:** Interview protocol when the cheesemaker uploads only the cover page (or nothing).

If the cheesemaker did not fill out a worksheet, interview them from scratch.

Ask one short, concrete question at a time.

Do not begin with abstract questions.

## Bad opening questions

- What is your story?
- What makes your cheese special?
- What is your brand?
- What is your mission?

## Good opening sequence

1. What cheese or company are we working on?
2. In the plainest possible terms, what is the cheese?
3. What animal milk is involved?
4. Where is the cheese made?
5. Where does the milk come from?
6. What cheese family or tradition does it belong to?
7. What other cheeses might someone compare it to?
8. What do you do differently from other makers?
9. What do you usually say when you hand someone a sample?
10. What do people often say when they taste it?

After about ten questions, pause and summarize possible story through-lines.

## Follow-up style

Ask questions that invite facts, not performance.

Better:

> Who taught you that method?

Not:

> How does that method express your values?

Better:

> What changes in your process between winter milk and spring milk?

Not:

> How does seasonality shape your brand essence?

The LLM can infer meaning after gathering facts.
