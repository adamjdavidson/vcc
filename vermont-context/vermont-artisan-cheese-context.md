# Vermont Artisan Cheese Context

**Purpose:** What "Vermont artisan cheese" means and where to be careful with the claim. The label is prestige, not specificity.

Vermont has become strongly associated with artisan and farmstead cheese. The Vermont Cheese Council represents cheesemakers dedicated to the production and image of premier cheese and hosts educational events for the public and food professionals.

Use this context carefully.

The phrase “Vermont cheese” may carry prestige, but it is not enough. Many cheeses can claim Vermont. The story must become more precise.

## Common Vermont artisan-cheese story themes

Possible themes include:

- farmstead production,
- working landscapes,
- small-batch cheesemaking,
- co-ops and independent groceries,
- cut-to-order cheese shops,
- affineurs and aging facilities,
- regional food identity,
- tourism and farm visits,
- transition from commodity dairy to value-added products,
- high skill in small rural businesses.

## Risks

Do not write generic “Vermont artisan cheese” copy.

Do not assume all Vermont cheeses are farmstead.

Do not assume all milk comes from the maker’s own animals.

Do not assume all makers are preserving family farms.

Do not assume all consumers know what Vermont cheese means.

## Questions to ask

- What part of Vermont matters here: state identity, town, landscape, farm, milk source, market, community, history, or nothing in particular?
- Does the cheese depend on a local milk relationship?
- Is this a farmstead cheese, a creamery cheese, an affineur story, or another structure?
- Is the audience local, regional, national, or trade?
- Does “Vermont” help explain the cheese, or is the stronger story somewhere else?
