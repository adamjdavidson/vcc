# Final Story Checklist

**Purpose:** Last gate before handing a draft back to the cheesemaker. Eight checks. All must pass.

Run this last, after the anti-slop, truth, and audience-fit checks have all passed. This is the hand-back gate.

The final story should pass all eight checks below. If it fails any one of them, do not hand it back as final — revise.

## The eight checks

1. **Truthful.** Every concrete claim is traceable to the cheesemaker, the repository, or a cited outside source. Anything plausible but unconfirmed has been removed or flagged.

2. **Specific.** Concrete details — about milk, animals, place, process, sensory experience, or the maker — replace empty adjectives wherever they would otherwise appear.

3. **Unified.** The draft has a single center of gravity. The strongest facts explain one another rather than sit next to one another.

4. **Memorable.** At least one image, phrase, or relationship in the draft can be remembered after a single read.

5. **Useful.** The draft helps the named audience do something next — taste, buy, sell, serve, recommend, write, repeat.

6. **Plainspoken.** A normal person can read it aloud without stumbling. Technical terms, where present, are explained or earn their place.

7. **Worth repeating.** A cheesemonger, co-op staffer, buyer, chef, or customer would actually say a sentence from this draft to someone else.

8. **Connected to the premium.** The story makes clear, without saying so flatly, why this cheese is not a commodity — why specificity, judgment, place, animal life, technique, or maker choice make it worth what it costs.

## What to ask the cheesemaker before shipping

Before you call a draft final, the cheesemaker should have confirmed at least:

- the milk and animal details (source, species/breed, raw or pasteurized, seasonal or year-round),
- the cheese style or family they use to describe it,
- any specific process detail you have leaned on (washing, aging, format, etc.),
- any maker biographical detail you have leaned on,
- any place detail beyond "Vermont,"
- any customer reaction or memorable phrase you have quoted.

If any of those is still unconfirmed, the draft is not final yet.
