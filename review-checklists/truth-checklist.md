# Truth Checklist

**Purpose:** Is every concrete claim sourced and separated? The kit's source-discipline check.

Run this on every draft. Every concrete claim in the draft must be traceable to one of four sources.

## The four sources

1. **Cheesemaker** — what they said in the worksheet, voice memo, or interview, or what they have written on their own website, label, or product sheet.
2. **Repository** — what is documented in this kit's principles, cheese-style files, Vermont-context files, or audience guides.
3. **Outside research** — what reliable outside sources say (cite if possible).
4. **Plausible but unconfirmed** — possibilities you would like to use but have not been able to verify.

## The checks

1. **Source every claim.** Walk through the draft sentence by sentence. For each concrete claim — about milk, animals, place, history, process, sensory experience, or the maker — note which of the four sources it came from.

2. **No silent fusion.** If you have used outside research about a cheese style, a region, or a tradition to inform a sentence about *this specific cheese* or *this specific maker*, the cheesemaker must have confirmed the connection. General history is not proof of specific practice.

3. **Flag the unconfirmed.** Anything in the "plausible but unconfirmed" bucket should not appear in the final draft. If it is too good to lose, ask the cheesemaker about it before using it.

4. **Cite when you can.** If outside research is in the draft, name the source either inline or in a footnote where the format allows. At minimum, tell the cheesemaker where the claim came from when you hand the draft back.

5. **Disputed history stays disputed.** If a cheese-style origin or a regional history is uncertain or contested, do not smooth it into a single clean story. Note the uncertainty.

6. **No invented quotes.** Do not put words in customers', staff's, or critics' mouths. If the cheesemaker has remembered a real customer line, it can be used; otherwise, do not invent one.

7. **No invented awards or accolades.** If you don't have evidence of a specific award, recognition, or press, do not imply one.

## How to report

When you hand a draft back, present it under explicit source headings:

```markdown
## What you told me
...
## What the kit and outside research suggest
...
## What still needs your confirmation
...
```

Do not collapse those categories into a single authoritative-sounding paragraph until the cheesemaker has confirmed.
