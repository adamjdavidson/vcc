# Audience Research

**Purpose:** How to think about audience for a cheese story — adapt emphasis, not invent disconnected narratives.

A cheese story should be audience-aware without becoming fake.

Different audiences may need different versions of the same story.

Do not create disconnected stories for each audience. Start with the strongest unifying narrative, then adapt it.

## Questions to ask about audience

- Who needs to understand this cheese?
- Who needs to repeat the story when the maker is not present?
- Is the story for an end consumer, a cheesemonger, a buyer, a chef, a wholesaler, or press?
- What does that audience already know?
- What might they misunderstand?
- What decision are they making?
- What language would sound natural in that setting?

## Audience goals

Consumers need to feel invited and curious.

Cheesemongers need memorable language they can repeat quickly.

Retail buyers need category fit, price support, differentiation, and staff-useful language.

Wholesalers and distributors need a portable story that survives distance.

Chefs need use cases, menu fit, flavor, format, and reliability.

Food writers need a real narrative, not a pitch.

## Avoid pandering

Do not flatten the story to suit an audience.

Adapt emphasis, vocabulary, and length. Keep the underlying story truthful.
